For Those Experiencing Bullying

Remember: It's not your fault, and you deserve to feel safe and happy.

  • Tell someone you trust about what's happening
  • Keep a record of bullying incidents
  • Stay close to friends and supportive people
  • Remember your worth isn't determined by bullies

For Parents

Signs your child might be experiencing bullying:

  • Sudden changes in behavior or mood
  • Reluctance to go to school
  • Unexplained injuries or damaged belongings
  • Changes in eating or sleeping habits

For Friends Who Want to Help

  • Stand up for your friends when you see bullying
  • Include others who might feel left out
  • Listen without judgment
  • Encourage them to seek help from trusted adults

Types of Bullying

Physical Bullying

Includes hitting, pushing, taking belongings, or any form of physical intimidation.

  • Hitting or pushing
  • Taking or damaging belongings
  • Physical intimidation
  • Threatening gestures

Verbal Bullying

Using words to hurt or humiliate others.

  • Name-calling
  • Insults and teasing
  • Threatening
  • Discriminatory remarks

Cyberbullying

Bullying that takes place online or through digital devices.

  • Social media harassment
  • Harmful messages or emails
  • Sharing private information
  • Online exclusion

Social Bullying

Damaging someone's social reputation or relationships.

  • Spreading rumors
  • Social exclusion
  • Public humiliation
  • Manipulating friendships
